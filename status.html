Current Status

SOE materials are working papers, architecture documents, source archives, and architecture-model simulation evidence. They are suitable for research review, critique, and further development.

They do not present empirical validation, pilot readiness, deployment readiness, cryptographic readiness, real-world node recognition, or Operation Layer freeze.

Evidence Classes

Architecture documents

Formal structure proposals, revisions, and public working-paper snapshots.

Architecture-model simulation evidence

Outputs from bounded internal simulations. These can support model-level structural claims but do not establish real-world validation.

Source archives

Supporting materials, review packets, datasets, and source collections that preserve context for later inspection.

Scenario seeds

Fiction and media materials used to surface imagined failure modes. They are not evidence that the scenarios are likely or empirically present.

Concern inputs

Nonfiction and expert-source findings that require mapping before they can affect architecture claims.

Archival public version

A public Zenodo record preserved for citation and inspection. Later versions may revise framing or structure without erasing the prior record.

Claim Boundary

The public archive separates simulation-supported architecture claims from empirical, policy, deployment, or operational claims. A record may say that a model, paper design, or internal simulation produced a result. It may not treat that result as proof of real-world governance performance.

When a page uses terms such as evidence, validation, readiness, or operation, the intended meaning is bounded by the evidence class listed on that page and by the associated Zenodo record.
